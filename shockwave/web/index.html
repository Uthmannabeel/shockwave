The blind spot

Every reviewer has approved a one-line change that quietly broke something three files away. The honest question in review — “if I change this, what actually breaks?” — is one grep can't answer. The answer lives in the resolved call graph, not in text. GitLab Orbit already built that graph. Shockwave turns it into the answer.

How it works

From a change to its consequences.

Three steps, no setup beyond indexing your repo. Shockwave never guesses — it only reports what the graph returns.

01 · SEED

Pick the epicenter

Point at a function, class, file, or a whole merge-request diff — shockwave analyze process_payment.

02 · TRAVERSE

Walk the Orbit graph

A cycle-safe traversal of inbound CALLS and EXTENDS edges finds everything that transitively depends on the change.

03 · REPORT

Rank, expose, suggest

Risk-ranked impact, the public entry points it reaches, and a generated test stub for every untested hotspot.

Why a knowledge graph

grep finds text. Orbit knows your code.

Text search

  • Matches a name, not a reference
  • Misses cross-file and inherited callers
  • No notion of distance, risk, or coverage
  • Can't tell internal from externally reachable

Shockwave + Orbit

  • Follows the resolved call & inheritance graph
  • Transitive, cycle-safe, with hop distance
  • Ranks risk and flags untested hotspots
  • Traces the path from a public entry point to the change
