How it works

From a change to its consequences.

Shockwave never guesses. It resolves your change to nodes in GitLab Orbit's knowledge graph, walks the real call and inheritance edges, and reports only what the graph returns.

01 · SEED

Pick the epicenter

A function, class, file, or a whole merge-request diff. Shockwave resolves it to one or more Definition nodes in the graph.

02 · TRAVERSE

Walk the graph

A cycle-safe breadth-first walk over inbound CALLS and EXTENDS edges finds everything that transitively depends on the seed, with each node's hop distance.

03 · REPORT

Rank, expose, suggest

Risk-rank by fan-in × proximity, flag the public entry points the change reaches, and generate a test stub for every untested hotspot.

The substrate

A graph, not a grep.

GitLab Orbit indexes your repository into a knowledge graph: definitions, files, imports, and the resolved relationships between them. That resolution is the whole point — a call to process_payment in another module is a real edge, not a string match.

Shockwave reads that graph two ways: Orbit Local (a DuckDB graph on your machine) and Orbit Remote (the hosted graph, over the REST query API). Same analysis, same answers.

The algorithm

Bounded, cycle-safe, exact.

Orbit Remote forbids unbounded queries, so the transitive radius is computed hop by hop: resolve the seed, then expand each frontier by node ids until it runs dry or hits the hop budget — deduping by id, tracking minimum distance, and reconstructing the call path back to the change.

🎯

Anchored

Every query is anchored by a filter or node ids. No full-table scans, ever.

🔁

Cycle-safe

Visited-set BFS with a hop bound — recursion and mutual calls terminate cleanly.

🧭

Path-aware

Parent pointers reconstruct the exact chain from a public entry point to the change.
